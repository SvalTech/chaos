Official Documentation

Master your daily chaos.

ChaosPrep is a quiet, distraction-free tracking system designed specifically for JEE, NEET, and competitive exam aspirants. No AI fluff, no social feeds—just raw productivity tools to help you hit your targets and visualize your growth.

Initial Setup

When you first sign in, configure your environment to match your specific goals. ChaosPrep automatically adapts its interface based on your choices.

  • Select your Exam Target: Open Settings (gear icon) and choose JEE Main, JEE Advanced, NEET, or Custom. This automatically filters your Syllabus and configures your Reality Check countdown.
  • Add Custom Subjects: Studying for boards or a state exam? Use the + Custom button in the Quick Add Task menu or Settings to add subjects like "English" or "CS".
  • Set your Target Year: Select the year you are appearing for the exam to ensure accurate timeline tracking.

Daily Planner & Subtasks

The Planner transforms broad syllabus goals into executable, bite-sized daily tasks.

Adding and Reordering Tasks

Click the + Add Task button on any day card. Write your objective and select a subject. To reorganize your day, click the specific Day Card to open the Day View, then grab the handle to drag and drop tasks into your preferred execution order.

Subtasks (Micro-Checklists)

Complex chapters need breaking down. Inside the Day View, click the "Add subtask..." input below any main task to create a nested checklist (e.g., Task: Rotational Motion -> Subtasks: Watch Lecture, Read NCERT, Solve 50 Qs).

Timer, PiP & Zen Mode

Clock actual deep work hours. The Timer view prevents you from lying to yourself about your productivity.

Full-Screen Zen Mode

Click the expand icon in the top right of the timer. This locks your device into a distraction-free landscape mode, hiding all menus and nav bars.

Picture-in-Picture (PiP)

On desktop, click the PiP icon to pop the timer out into a floating window that stays on top of your screen while you read PDFs or watch lectures.

  • Anti-Throttling: Unlike normal browser timers that pause when you switch tabs, ChaosPrep uses a background Web Worker and Screen Wake Locks to ensure your timer never loses a second.
  • Task Linking: Before starting the timer, use the dropdown to link an active task. When you stop studying, it will prompt you to mark that specific task as completed.
  • Focus Radio: Toggle the floating music icon to stream live Lofi, Synthwave, or Classical focus music directly via YouTube without needing a premium subscription.

Daily Questions Tracker

Theory is useless without execution. The Daily Questions tracker holds you accountable for your actual problem-solving volume.

  • Set Your Target: Click the target number (default 50) to set how many questions you aim to solve daily.
  • Log by Subject: Enter the exact number of questions solved per subject. The dashboard instantly tallies your total.
  • Maintain the Streak: Hitting your daily target extends your Flame Streak . Missing a day resets it to zero.

Mocks & Error Analytics

Data drives improvement. ChaosPrep separates your analytics into Mock Test tracking and detailed Error categorization.

Logging a Mock Test

To log a mock, add a task and select Mock as the subject. This unlocks special score input fields. Enter your obtained and max marks. Once saved, this data maps to the interactive trajectory chart in the Stats view.

Error Tracking (Mistake Profiles)

Don't just look at the final score; analyze the failure points. Log specific mistakes by Subject, Chapter, and Type:

  • Conceptual: You didn't know or misunderstood the core theory.
  • Calculative: You knew the method, but messed up the math/algebra.
  • Silly Mistake: Misread the question, marked the wrong bubble, unit conversion error, etc.
  • Formula: Forgot or misapplied a specific formula.

The system generates a dynamic "Mistake Profile" Doughnut chart so you know exactly which error types are bleeding your marks.

Syllabus Tracker

A comprehensive, pre-loaded list of chapters based on standard syllabus guidelines.

Instead of simple checkboxes, ChaosPrep uses a weighted phased approach:

To Do (0%) Doing (50%) Done (100%) Mastered (100%)

Expand any chapter to also track your Revision Count (R1, R2...) and toggle whether you've completed PYQs for that specific topic.

Squads (Multiplayer)

Studying for competitive exams is incredibly isolating. Squads is a multiplayer accountability feature designed to simulate the feeling of studying in a library with your hardest-working friends. See when they focus, share daily targets, and win together.

Building Your Squad

Unlike WhatsApp or Discord, there are no centralized "Group Links." Squads are built entirely through mutual, 1-on-1 connections. This prevents massive, distracting groups from forming.

  1. Find Your Code: Go to the Squad view. At the top, you will see your unique 6-character Invite Code.
  2. Share It: Click the code to copy it, or use the "Link" button to generate a direct invite URL.
  3. Mutual Connection: To form a squad of 3 people (You, Alice, and Bob), everyone must mutually add each other. You must add Alice, Alice must add Bob, and Bob must add You.

Live Status & The "Pulse"

The most powerful feature of Squads is live presence. We don't track location or keystrokes—we track focus state.

Studying

When a user turns on their Timer, their status instantly turns red. The pulse indicates deep focus.

Idle

The timer is off, but the user has interacted with the app within the last 15 minutes. Taking a break.

Offline

The user has closed the app or hasn't interacted for over 15 minutes. Shows "Seen X hrs ago".

Task Syncing (Privacy First)

By default, tasks scheduled for Today are visible to your Squad. As you check them off in your planner, they cross out on your friends' screens in real-time, creating positive peer pressure.

If you prefer to keep your to-do list private, go to Settings → Share Tasks and toggle it off. Your friends will still see your live study status, but your tasks will say "Tasks hidden".

Themes, Colors & Atmosphere

Make the workspace entirely yours. Access these in Settings:

  • Accent Themes: Not a fan of the default Purple? Switch your entire UI to Matcha (Green), Cobalt (Blue), Crimson (Red), or Monochrome (Minimalist B&W).
  • Subject Colors: Assign custom colors (Rose, Amber, Emerald, Cyan, etc.) to specific subjects. These colors reflect across your planner tags and stat charts.
  • Atmosphere: Choose a preset background (Minimal, Space, Ocean) or paste a custom image URL to set an immersive wallpaper that sits behind the frosted glass UI.
  • Lite Mode: Running ChaosPrep on a budget device? Toggle Lite Mode to disable heavy glass blurs, shadows, and animations to save battery and boost speed.

Day Rollover (Night Owls)

By default, calendar apps reset at 12:00 AM. If you are a student who studies until 3:00 AM, having your planner jump to "Tomorrow" mid-session is frustrating and breaks your streaks.

The Fix: Change your Day Rollover setting. If set to 4:00 AM, ChaosPrep considers it "Today" until 3:59 AM. Your tasks, streaks, and charts won't reset until you actually go to sleep.

Data & Calendar Export

Your data belongs to you. In Settings, you can manage it freely:

  • Export to Calendar (.ics): Generates a standard calendar file containing all your past and future tasks. You can import this into Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, or Outlook to view your study plan alongside your life events.
  • JSON Backup/Restore: Export your entire database (tasks, logs, settings, syllabus) as a local `.json` file. You can import this file later to restore your exact state across devices or accounts.

Reality Check

Clicking the countdown pill in the sidebar/header opens the Reality Check. It's designed to be brutal.

It calculates exactly how many days, "sleeps", and Sundays you have left until your exam. It also calculates your Wasted Potential—showing exactly how many total hours of prep time you will lose before the exam if you waste just 2 hours every day. No excuses.
